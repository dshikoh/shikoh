Sound for Film & Location Recording

Practice · Location sound · Post & sound design

I approach location sound as a form of authorship: listening is not only technical capture, but a way of shaping narrative, space, and intimacy. My work combines precision with care for context—especially when working across cultures, institutions, and communities.

My practice is informed by research and ethnographic listening, with a focus on sonic sensitivity, migration, and decolonial narratives. Whether recording on set or shaping sound in post, I treat sound as an active storytelling layer rather than a background element.
